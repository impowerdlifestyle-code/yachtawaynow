The Journey

The Route to the Bahamas

The most popular route from Tampa Bay to the Bahamas follows the Florida coastline south to Key West, then crosses the Gulf Stream to Bimini — the closest Bahamian island to the U.S. mainland at just 50 miles from the Florida coast. The journey down the Keys is an adventure in itself, with opportunities to stop in Naples, Marathon, or Key West along the way.

A typical trip takes two to three days each way, depending on your pace and how many stops you want to make. Some guests prefer to cruise straight through with overnight stops at marinas along the Keys. Others turn the journey into the vacation itself, spending a night in Key West before making the crossing to Bimini. From Bimini, you can island-hop through the Bahamas at your leisure — the Abacos, Eleuthera, Nassau, or the Exumas are all within reach.

For those who prefer a shorter crossing, a direct route from Key West to Bimini takes approximately four to five hours in favorable conditions. Your captain monitors weather patterns and Gulf Stream conditions to select the safest and most comfortable crossing window. Every route is planned with safety margins built in, including fuel reserves and contingency stops.

Island Life

What to Expect in the Bahamas

Once you arrive in Bahamian waters, the real magic begins. Bimini is the gateway — a laid-back island with world-class bone fishing, incredible snorkeling at the Bimini Road (an underwater rock formation some believe is part of the lost city of Atlantis), and beachside restaurants serving fresh conch and cold Kalik beer. Spend a day or two here before pushing further into the chain.

From Bimini, many guests head to the Abacos, a boater's paradise of sheltered cays, colorful colonial towns, and some of the clearest water on earth. Anchor in a protected harbor, dinghy to shore for lunch at a waterfront cafe, then snorkel a coral reef teeming with tropical fish. The Berry Islands offer even more seclusion — private beaches where your footprints are the only ones in the sand.

Every day looks different on a Bahamas yacht charter. One morning you might wake up to dolphins playing off your bow. The next, you are snorkeling over a reef so vivid it looks artificial. Afternoons are spent on deserted beaches or exploring tiny settlements where the locals wave from their porches. Evenings bring spectacular sunsets, fresh-caught fish on the grill, and the kind of silence you simply cannot find on land. It is transformative travel at its finest.

Trip Planning

Planning Your Bahamas Adventure

The best time to charter to the Bahamas is April through July, when the Gulf Stream is calmest and the weather is most predictable. Spring offers the perfect combination of warm temperatures, low humidity, and gentle seas. Summer brings the warmest water for swimming and snorkeling, though your captain will monitor tropical weather closely.

Weather Windows

Your captain monitors marine forecasts daily and selects optimal crossing windows with calm seas and favorable winds. Safety always comes first, and we build flexible schedules to accommodate weather patterns.

Customs & Immigration

Clearing into the Bahamas is straightforward. Your captain handles all paperwork and procedures. You will need a valid passport and we will guide you through the customs process upon arrival in Bimini.

What to Pack

Light clothing, swimwear, reef-safe sunscreen, snorkel gear if you have it (we carry extras), a valid passport, and any medications. The galley stores provisions for the trip. We will help you create a packing and provisions list.

Provisions Planning

We help you plan meals and supplies for the entire trip. Stock up before departure at St. Pete markets, or we can recommend provisioning services. The full galley means you can prepare meals aboard or eat ashore at island restaurants.
