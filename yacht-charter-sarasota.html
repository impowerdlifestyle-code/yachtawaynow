Why Sarasota

Sarasota's Finest By Water

Sarasota is home to some of the most beautiful coastline in the United States — and the best way to experience it is from the deck of a private yacht. From the world-famous quartz-crystal sands of Siesta Key to the upscale charm of St. Armands Circle, Sarasota's waterfront is a destination unlike any other on Florida's Gulf Coast.

Departing from our St. Petersburg dock, we head south through the scenic Intracoastal Waterway toward Sarasota Bay. Along the route, you'll cruise past Anna Maria Island, under the Cortez Bridge, and along Longboat Key's pristine shoreline before reaching Sarasota's barrier islands. Pass through New Pass or Big Sarasota Pass into the open Gulf for swimming, anchoring, and unforgettable sunset views.

Siesta Key Access

Cruise to America's #1 beach. Anchor offshore and swim in the crystal-clear Gulf waters surrounding Siesta Key's legendary quartz-crystal sand.

Glide along Longboat Key's pristine shoreline through the Intracoastal Waterway. Miles of unspoiled beaches and luxurious waterfront estates line the route.

Navigate the calm, protected waters of Sarasota Bay. Spot dolphins, manatees, and sea birds as you cruise through one of Florida's most scenic bays.

Lido Key & St. Armands

Anchor near Lido Key and take in views of the St. Armands Circle waterfront. A perfect stop for photos, swimming, and soaking in Sarasota's upscale coastal vibe.

Where We'll Take You

From our St. Petersburg dock, Sarasota's barrier islands and bays are a scenic cruise south through the Intracoastal Waterway. Here's what awaits.

Siesta Key

Consistently ranked America's #1 beach, Siesta Key is famous for its ultra-fine quartz-crystal sand that stays cool underfoot. Approach from the water through Big Sarasota Pass for a perspective most visitors never see. Anchor offshore for swimming in the impossibly clear Gulf waters.

Longboat Key & New Pass

Longboat Key stretches over 10 miles between Sarasota Bay and the Gulf of Mexico. Cruise its Intracoastal shoreline past luxury estates and pristine mangroves, then head through New Pass into the open Gulf for spectacular sunset views and deep-water swimming.

Lido Key & Sarasota Bay

Lido Key sits between Longboat Key and Siesta Key, offering beautiful beaches and views of the St. Armands Circle waterfront. Sarasota Bay itself is a calm, protected waterway ideal for cruising, with frequent dolphin and manatee sightings throughout the year.
