Common Questions

Yacht Charter Pricing FAQ

What's included in the charter price?

Weekend packages ($2,000–$2,500) are all-inclusive — captain, crew, and fuel are included with no hidden fees. Weekday hourly charters ($300/hr) have a 4-hour minimum with a $50/hr fuel surcharge and $100/hr captain fee added separately. All charters include full vessel access across 3 decks, 2 cabins, premium stereo with Bluetooth, and capacity for up to 13 guests.

Do I need to bring my own food and drinks?

Yes — all charters are BYOB. You're welcome to bring your own food, drinks, and alcohol aboard. There's a full galley kitchen, refrigerator, and plenty of cooler space. Many guests pick up catering from local St. Petersburg restaurants before boarding. Need suggestions? Just ask when you book.

How far in advance should I book?

We recommend booking at least 2–3 weeks in advance, especially for weekends and holidays. Peak season (March through October) fills up fast. For holidays like July 4th, Gasparilla, and New Year's Eve, book 4–6 weeks ahead to secure your date. A 50% deposit is required to confirm your reservation.

What is the cancellation policy?

Cancellations made 72+ hours before departure receive a full refund of the deposit. Cancellations within 72 hours are non-refundable. If we cancel due to unsafe weather conditions, you'll receive a full refund or the option to reschedule at no extra cost. Your safety is always our top priority.

Can I customize the route or itinerary?

Absolutely. Every charter is private and fully customizable. Popular routes include sunset cruises past the St. Pete Pier and Vinoy, island hops to Egmont Key and Shell Key, sandbar stops at Fort De Soto, and passes under the Skyway Bridge. Your captain will work with you to plan the perfect route based on weather, tides, and your preferences.

Is gratuity expected for the captain and crew?

Gratuity is not included in the charter price and is entirely at your discretion. If you had a great experience, most guests tip 15–20% of the charter total. You can tip in cash at the end of your trip or add it when completing payment.
