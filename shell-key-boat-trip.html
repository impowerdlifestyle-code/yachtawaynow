The Shell Key Experience

Secluded Island Paradise

Shell Key Preserve is a pristine, undeveloped barrier island just south of St. Pete Beach — and one of the most beautiful natural destinations on Florida's Gulf Coast. Protected as part of the Pinellas County Aquatic Preserve, this island has no roads, no buildings, and no crowds. It's the kind of place that feels like a private paradise, and aboard our 52ft Marquis Flybridge, that's exactly what it becomes.

Shell Key is famous for its world-class shelling, with conch shells, sand dollars, and sea glass scattered along miles of untouched shoreline. The surrounding shallow sandbars are perfect for wading, swimming, and simply soaking in the turquoise water. Dolphin encounters are common here — pods regularly cruise through the channels around the island. During nesting season, you may even spot sea turtles along the shore. Shell Key is approximately 20 minutes from our dock, and can easily be combined with Pass-a-Grille or Egmont Key for an unforgettable full-day island-hopping charter.

World-Class Shelling

Shell Key lives up to its name. Discover conch shells, sand dollars, sea glass, and countless other treasures along miles of untouched shoreline that few visitors ever reach.

Dolphin Encounters

Pods of bottlenose dolphins regularly cruise through the channels surrounding Shell Key. Watch them play in the wake or swim alongside the yacht on your approach to the island.

Pristine Sandbars

Shallow, crystal-clear sandbars surround Shell Key — perfect for wading, swimming, and lounging in knee-deep turquoise water. It's the definition of a Florida postcard moment.

Sea Turtle Habitat (seasonal)

During nesting season, Shell Key becomes a critical habitat for sea turtles. Witness one of nature's most incredible cycles on this protected barrier island preserve.

What to Expect

Everything you need to know about your private Shell Key boat trip from St. Petersburg.

The Voyage

Shell Key is approximately a 20-minute cruise from our St. Petersburg dock. The short, scenic ride takes you past Pass-a-Grille and through the calm intracoastal waters of Boca Ciega Bay. Dolphins are frequently spotted along the route.

Your Vessel

Our 52ft Marquis Flybridge accommodates up to 13 guests across 3 expansive decks. Climate-controlled salon, premium high-quality stereo, full galley, and 2 cabins. BYOB — bring your own food and drinks for the perfect island day.

Island Combo Options

Make it a full-day adventure by combining Shell Key with Pass-a-Grille or Egmont Key. Hop between islands, explore multiple sandbars, and experience more of Florida's Gulf Coast barrier islands — all in one private charter.
